How predictions work

The big number is the official LTA estimate. Underneath it, the AI estimate shows when the bus will actually arrive based on how this exact route at this exact stop has behaved historically.

Two prediction layers work together: a gradient-boosting model that generalises across hour, day, crowding and bus type — and a per-route memory of real measured delays from the last 30 days. Routes with more history lean on real measurements; the correction also shrinks as a bus gets close, since live GPS estimates are most accurate then.

Ground truth comes from tracking each bus across successive polls until it arrives. The model retrains itself hourly.
